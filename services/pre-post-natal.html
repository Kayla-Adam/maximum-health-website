Pre & Post Natal Massage

Specialized massage for expecting and new mothers, addressing pregnancy-related discomfort and supporting postpartum recovery with safe, adapted techniques.

Pre and post natal massage is a specialized form of therapy designed to support women through the remarkable physical and emotional changes of pregnancy and the postpartum period. At Maximum Health Massage Therapy Calgary, our registered massage therapists are specifically trained in the safe positioning, pressure modifications, and techniques required to provide effective and comfortable care for expecting and new mothers. We understand that every pregnancy is unique, and we tailor each session to address your individual needs and stage of pregnancy or recovery.

Pregnancy places significant demands on the body — shifting your centre of gravity, increasing the load on your joints and spine, and creating hormonal changes that affect your muscles and ligaments. Postpartum recovery brings its own challenges, from the physical demands of caring for a newborn to the body's process of returning to its pre-pregnancy state. Massage therapy provides safe, drug-free relief for the aches and discomforts that accompany both stages, helping you feel more comfortable, rested, and supported during one of the most important times of your life.

Benefits of Pre & Post Natal Massage

  • Back and hip pain relief: Addresses the lower back pain, sacroiliac dysfunction, and hip discomfort that commonly develop as your body adapts to carrying additional weight and a shifting centre of gravity.
  • Reduced swelling: Gentle techniques help move excess fluid from swollen ankles, feet, and hands — a common concern especially in the second and third trimesters.
  • Better sleep: Eases the physical discomfort and mental tension that often interfere with restful sleep during pregnancy and the early postpartum weeks.
  • Stress and anxiety reduction: Promotes the release of serotonin and dopamine while reducing cortisol levels, helping to manage the emotional ups and downs that often accompany hormonal changes.
  • Postpartum recovery: Supports your body's return to its pre-pregnancy state by addressing muscle imbalances, tension from breastfeeding and baby-carrying postures, and general fatigue.
  • Hormonal balance support: Regular massage during pregnancy has been shown to help regulate hormones associated with relaxation and stress, contributing to a healthier pregnancy experience overall.

What to Expect During Your Session

Your comfort and safety are our top priorities. During prenatal massage, you will be positioned on your side using supportive pillows and bolsters to ensure both you and your baby are comfortable and secure throughout the treatment. This side-lying position is the safest and most comfortable option, especially as your pregnancy progresses. Your therapist will use gentle, adapted techniques that are appropriate for your current trimester, avoiding any pressure points or areas that are contraindicated during pregnancy.

Treatment focuses on the areas that typically cause the most discomfort during pregnancy — the lower back, hips, legs, and shoulders — while also providing overall relaxation and stress relief. For postpartum sessions, techniques are adapted to address the specific challenges of recovery, including upper back and neck tension from feeding and holding your baby, abdominal and core restoration, and general fatigue. Your therapist will modify the treatment plan as your body changes throughout pregnancy and into the postpartum period, ensuring that every session is safe, effective, and supportive.

Who Is Pre & Post Natal Massage For?

Pre and post natal massage is designed for pregnant women in all trimesters (with the approval of their healthcare provider), new mothers navigating the physical demands of postpartum recovery, and women experiencing pregnancy-related aches and pains such as sciatica, round ligament pain, carpal tunnel syndrome, and general muscle tension. If you are expecting or have recently welcomed a new baby, this gentle and nurturing treatment can make a meaningful difference in how you feel during this transformative time. We recommend discussing massage therapy with your doctor or midwife, and our therapists are happy to coordinate with your healthcare team to ensure the best possible care.
